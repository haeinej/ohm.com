01
Design Principles
ohm. has one visual job: make a thought feel worth stopping for. Every decision — layout, colour, type, spacing — serves that single moment.
The feed is warm, quiet, analogue in feeling. It does not perform energy. Cards sit on warm off-white. Images are desaturated and cinematic. Type is minimal and restrained. The product recedes so the thought can come forward.
The design recedes. The thought comes forward.
PRINCIPLES
Background feel
	Warm off-white — paper, not screen. Never clinical white, never pure black.
	Image treatment
	Desaturated, low contrast, ~70% brightness. Film still, not social card.
	Type register
	Comico + Sentient only. Light weights. Minimal size range. Nothing decorative.
	Colour role
	Depth (panels darkening) and warmth (connection forming). Never decorative.
	Accent
	#EB4101 — one colour, specific meaning only.
	Ground
	#979C5B — earthen stability, the olive that holds everything.
	Motion
	Gestural. Swipe left deeper, swipe right back. No bounce, no snap.

02
Colour System
Colour communicates exactly two things: depth (which panel you are on) and warmth (whether a connection is forming). It is never used decoratively.
The palette draws from the earth. Vermillion is fired clay — the heat of connection. Olive is terroir — the ground everything grows from. Chartreuse is new growth — the brightness of a fresh thought. These are colours with origins, not choices from a picker.

03
Typography
Two typefaces. One system. Comico for all display and structural text — headings, the logo wordmark, section numbers, navigation. Sentient for all reading text — thought sentences, context, replies, body copy. Both from Fontshare, both free for commercial use.
Comico is organic, expressive, and bold — it carries the same energy as the logomark. Sentient is warm, literate, and restrained — it recedes so the thought comes forward. Together they create the tension between expression and quiet that defines ohm.
No italic. No third typeface. If a design requires something else to work, the design is wrong.
TYPE SCALE
Thought sentence — P1
	Sentient Light · 12–13px · white · no background box
	Context — P2
	Sentient Light · 9–10px · muted white
	Reply input — P3
	Sentient Light · 11–12px
	Names / labels
	Comico · 7–8px · UPPERCASE · wide tracking
	Card metadata
	Comico · 7px · muted · UPPERCASE
	Section numbers
	Comico · 10–12px · muted
	Logo wordmark
	Comico Bold · lowercase · vermillion period
	TYPE PAIRING RATIONALE
Comico’s organic, hand-drawn character echoes the logomark — both share the same DNA of flowing, natural forms. It gives the brand its distinctive voice in small, structural moments: a section header, a navigation label, the wordmark itself.
Sentient provides the counterweight. As a refined serif designed for extended reading, it creates the quiet space where thoughts actually live. The pairing is deliberate: the brand speaks in Comico, the people speak in Sentient.

04
Logo System
The logo system has two components: the organic logomark and the wordmark. They work independently and together.
THE LOGOMARK
 Organic flowing form in vermillion 

The logomark is an organic, flowing form in vermillion (#EB4101). Its shapes are inspired by natural growth — a plant reaching, a hand opening, the moment before two forms connect. It is alive, not geometric. It breathes.
On its own, the logomark sits on olive (#979C5B) ground. This is its natural habitat. In product, it may appear at reduced opacity or in single-colour treatments, but never distorted, outlined, or reconstructed.
THE WORDMARK
ohm.
ohm. — Comico Bold, lowercase. The period is always vermillion (#EB4101). The letters are always dark (#1A1A16) on light grounds or white on dark grounds. The period never changes colour.
SYMBOL SYSTEM
Three marks, one family:
Ω  The omega symbol — the literal ohm sign. Beautiful as a logomark reference. Already iconic, already recognized, newly recontextualized.
◎  Concentric circle — resonance radiating outward. Used as a secondary graphic element.
ohm.  The wordmark itself — the primary brand identifier.
BRAND GRADIENT
Fired clay to weathered bronze. #EB4101 → #979C5B → #D0D37C. Ancient and grounded. Used in the logo environment, marketing materials, and cover treatments. Never inside the product UI.

06
Feed Layout
The feed is a vertical stack of thought cards on warm off-white (#F5F0EA). At any scroll position the user sees approximately 2.5 cards — one fully in view, the next partially visible, a sliver of the third. Partial visibility creates ambient pull without forcing movement.
Free scroll. No snap-to-card. The user moves at their own pace.
The card should look like a film still with a caption. If it reads as a social media post, the design is wrong.

07
Thought Panels
Every card has three panels. Swipe left to go deeper. Swipe right to go back. Horizontal slide — no fade, no bounce. Each panel occupies the full screen. Each is darker than the one before.
PANEL 1 — THE FRONT
Background: #0C0C0A beneath image. Image: full width, 4:3, desaturated landscape. AI-generated from sentence + profile photo. Sentence: Sentient Light 12–13px, white, bottom, no box. Profile photo + name below image. Left edge bar reflects engagement warmth.
PANEL 2 — THE BACK
Background: solid #0C0C0A. Sentence repeats at top, slightly smaller — anchors the reader. Context: up to 3 sentences, Sentient Light 9–10px, muted white. Hard limit: 3 sentences.
PANEL 3 — REPLIES
Background: #080604 — deepest panel. Top: accepted replies. Each shows profile photo, name, reply text. Bottom: text input. Label “reply” in #EB4101. Placeholder: “what this thought surfaces in you...”
Typing: left edge bar turns full vermillion the moment the user starts typing. Sent: brief haptic + visual pulse. Returns to Panel 1.
A reply is a thought, not a reaction. The friction of writing is correct.

09
Notification Panel
Not a separate screen. Slides down inline from the top of Screen 1 when the vermillion circle is tapped. The feed below shifts down to accommodate it.
Each row: replier’s profile photo + name + first words of reply + which thought they replied to. Two actions per row: Accept — Ignore.
Accept: reply visible on Panel 3. Conversation created in Screen 2. Replier sees no special notification. Ignore: dismissed silently. Replier receives no signal.
No counter. No label. The vermillion circle is the only indicator.
________________


10
Crossing & Shift
Two collaborative artefacts available after 10 or more exchanges. Both are quiet — findable, never prompted. Both require both people to complete.
CROSSING — WHERE TWO THOUGHTS MET
Joint thought: sentence + up to 3 sentences context + AI image from both profile photos. Both write a sentence version. Non-initiator chooses or writes new. Context written jointly in alternating turns, up to 3 sentences total. Lives only on both profiles. Never in the feed.
SHIFT — WHERE THINKING MOVED
Two-halved card: Before → After for each person, side by side. Each half: profile photo + before sentence (muted) + after sentence (full). No AI image. The two arcs are the content. Appears in both people’s Worlds feed. The conversation always stays private.
The crossing is where two thoughts met. The shift is proof that something moved.

12
Non-Negotiables
These decisions must survive the build. If a technical constraint creates pressure to change one of them, flag it before changing it.
Replies require acceptance before they appear
A reply waits in the notification centre until accepted. This is the gate that keeps the product from becoming a comment platform. Do not remove it, even under time pressure.
No metrics exposed to users
No counts of any kind. The left-edge warmth bar is the only engagement signal, and it is visual, not numerical.
No editing after posting
A thought is final when posted. Editing would retroactively change what someone responded to.
Replies must be written text
No preset replies, no emojis as primary action, no like button. If a user cannot find words, they do not reply. That friction is correct.
Dormancy is valid
A conversation going quiet is not a failure state. No automated nudges, no reconnect suggestions.
The interests field is not a skills field
It asks what the user is into right now. Not professional skills. Not a job title. Never use the word “skills” near it.
The crossing is private
Never in the feed, never in discovery, never visible to anyone outside the conversation it came from.
The shift conversation stays private
The shift appears in the feed. The thread never does. Only the arc — before and after — is ever visible.
Neither crossing nor shift is ever prompted
The options exist quietly after 10 exchanges. People find them when ready, or they don’t.


Post a thought. Someone replies. You accept. You talk.
